<p>The “KitKat theft” incident where a bulk shipment of KitKat bars was reportedly stolen and quickly became a viral talking point was not, at its origin, a marketing campaign.</p><p>It was a news moment. But what followed is what matters.</p><p>Brands, creators, and even unrelated companies immediately began producing content around it. Memes, commentary, playful takes on “taking a break,” and clever spins on the KitKat slogan flooded feeds.</p><p>None of this content was pre-planned.</p><p>None of it came from a quarterly content calendar.<strong> It was reactive.</strong></p><figure><img alt="" src="https://cdn-images-1.medium.com/max/404/0*NzauqyTdgSHMDVaF.jpg" /><figcaption>If found, please call the authorities.</figcaption></figure><p>Traditional social media marketing operates on a predictable structure. Brands define pillars, build calendars, and distribute content at regular intervals. The assumption is that consistency builds familiarity, and familiarity builds trust.</p><p>Interest-based marketing flips that model.</p><p>Instead of asking, “What should we post this week?” it asks, “What is our audience already thinking about today?”</p><p>The KitKat theft moment created a shared cultural context. People were already engaged. They were already curious. They were already talking.</p><p>This is the core principle: <strong>attention is not created, it is captured.</strong></p><p>Algorithms prioritize content that aligns with user behavior in real time. They do not reward brand loyalty; they reward relevance signals. Watch time, shares, comments, and saves all indicate that content matches user interest.</p><p>This means your content is no longer competing with other brands. It is competing with <em>everything.</em></p><p>The KitKat theft was not just a viral story. It was a reminder that attention is earned in the moment, not scheduled in advance.</p><p>So do something about it at your next B2B Marketing weekly meeting, break the pattern, have a KitKat.</p><img src="https://medium.com/_/stat?event=post.clientViewed&referrerSource=full_rss&postId=28ccc3ec6751" width="1" height="1" alt="">

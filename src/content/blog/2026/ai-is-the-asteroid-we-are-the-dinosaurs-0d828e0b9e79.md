<h3>AI is the asteroid and we are the dinosaurs.</h3><p>Isn’t it refreshing to know you’re reading something written by hand and not a bot?</p><figure><img alt="" src="https://cdn-images-1.medium.com/max/940/0*0NyIsX8leFfIyZMm.jpg" /><figcaption>Buckle up for a wild ride</figcaption></figure><p>Over the years, we’ve been so consumed with revenue generation, product differentiation and cost reduction that we have yet to discuss why we are doing this. It reminds me of the scene from the original Jurassic Park where Dr. Ian Malcolm (played by humanity’s best Jeff Goldblum) says “<strong>Your scientists were so preoccupied with whether or not they could, they didn’t stop to think if they should”.</strong></p><p>I think we’ve reached that point.</p><p>First, supervised learning came for repetitive tasks. Then unsupervised learning came for repetitive jobs. Agentic AI directly reduces the need to hire humans. AI products reduce the need for mid-level management. And the moment we achieve ASI (Artificial Superintelligence) we have to ask ourselves the question <strong>“do we need humans”?</strong></p><p>Now if you’re a human being asked this question, you’ll say yes, but what if you’re a robot? What if you only need electricity, access to data, and access to the physical world? <strong>Then the answer is flat out no.</strong></p><figure><img alt="" src="https://cdn-images-1.medium.com/max/912/0*1cJckvDG6bwSlypa" /><figcaption>You’re Jennifer Lawrence, I’m Leonardo DiCaprio. The roles will change by the end of this article.</figcaption></figure><p>Let’s think back to the “robotic laws” by Isaac Asimov. Movie reference is iRobot for those of you binge watchers out there reading this opinionated rant. Isaac Asimov’s Three Laws of Robotics are as follows:</p><ol><li>A robot may not injure a human being or, through inaction, allow a human being to come to harm.</li><li>A robot must obey orders given it by human beings except where such orders would conflict with the First Law.</li><li>A robot must protect its own existence as long as such protection does not conflict with the First or Second Law.</li></ol><p><strong>Ladies and gentlemen, it’s time to say out loud that Isaac Asimov’s imagination has failed him and now it’s our turn to act.</strong></p><p>Because none of these laws protect human’s ability to earn a living wage. It does not protect mass spread unemployment. It does not protect autonomous AI creating autonomous robots creating autonomous factories and offices. If you’re a shareholder, this will make you happy. If you’re in the working class, your future looks bleak.</p><figure><img alt="" src="https://cdn-images-1.medium.com/max/1024/0*OtCPJQaKLTxyCMLl.jpeg" /><figcaption>Take a deep breath, there’s still more to unpack.</figcaption></figure><p>Let me start by attacking all the geniuses that says AI won’t replace humans, it’ll replace jobs. <strong>Having a job is proof that you are human.</strong></p><p>Elon Musk at Davos said that “work will become optional”. Oh really?</p><p>So a child that spends over a decade in school, then enters higher education and their family makes the investment so that they may prosper is now automated because it’s optional? Are we saying that our purpose to exist, to create, to invest, to ideate and explore is now optional? This is something we need to ask ourselves now before the asteroid hits planet Earth.</p><p><strong>Is humanity optional?</strong> Because it seems that the richest people on the planet believe it is.</p><p>When ServiceNow CEO, Bill McDermott, said live on TV that “unemployment rate for new graduates will go from 9% to 30%”, why was there no revolution or protest? I would imagine every university and college faculty members would find this outrageous that AI is completely eliminating their existence. If new graduates are no longer required, then by direct correlation higher education is no longer required. This is a large American employer openly saying that employment will decrease and they are actively playing a role in it and this is not headline news.</p><figure><img alt="" src="https://cdn-images-1.medium.com/max/1000/0*91C33FxQ9loJWdBp" /><figcaption>Using memes in corporate articles is straight fire.</figcaption></figure><p>Fill 2 pots with water. One pot has cold water and a frog. The second pot has hot water and is boiling. If we put a frog in the boiling water, it’ll jump out. If you turn up the heat on the first pot with the frog with cold water, it’ll soon reach boiling point and the frog will die. Guess which pot we’re currently in?</p><p>Enough hating on AI. How do we protect ourselves? Here’s the impact center where the asteroid hits Earth. We need governments to regulate AI laws to protect civilization. However our government’s are being lobbied and influenced by the richest people in the world who own all the AI infrastructure and are speeding ahead because of geopolitical competition. So the demand for AI regulation isn’t as high as the demand of AI.</p><p>Every person, company, country, and civilization has a critical point of action or inaction. A moment where a decision has to be made to course our path. In the Christopher Nolan movie Oppenheimer, a calculation resulted in a finding that there might be a remote chance that a nuclear bomb could possibly ignite the entire atmosphere. If the calculation’s were to be true, the US military would’ve been ready to share their findings with Germany to avoid the collapse of the entire planet.</p><p>What’s even more insane is that we don’t have to hide our findings. There is consensus globally about the ramification’s of poor AI regulations.</p><figure><img alt="" src="https://cdn-images-1.medium.com/max/1024/0*r4vBxABLD73PwLmn.jpeg" /><figcaption>Congratulations, you’re at the end?</figcaption></figure><p>In conclusion — you are Leonardo DiCaprio in the movie “Don’t Look Up”.</p><p>Are you going to voice your concerns to your elected representatives, to your managers, to your community, to highlight this issue that affects every single person you know or will you not look up at the asteroid and continue maximizing shareholder value until you are faced with your own job being eliminated and your existence being questioned?</p><p>What would a dinosaur do?</p><img src="https://medium.com/_/stat?event=post.clientViewed&referrerSource=full_rss&postId=0d828e0b9e79" width="1" height="1" alt="">
